Welcome to Safety Zone

In the final task (T3) of the discipline IA369, I buit an interactive visualization for inspecting the safety of the city of San Francisco. The application allows you to select a point in the map (please choose a point within San Francisco) and check if your surrounding is safe, a place to be careful or dangerous. The danger level is based on crimes that occurred within a given radius of the choosing location in 2016. The level of the threat is expressed by the filling color of the circle and an audio message.

Safety zone

How to use it

  • Just select a point in SF by clicking on the map and the safety zone will be calculated
  • Set the radius of the safety zone with the slider below the map. You can choose among the following distances: 100 m, 250 m, 500 m, 750 m and 1 km.
  • The application only has SF police incidents at its disposal. Thus, the only realiable results are those within SF borders.
  • In case you step out of San Francisco territory, you can press the button to recenter the map in SF

Rendering bug: If your browser is rendering white stripes inside the circle, press 'Ctrl' + '0'.

Choose safety radius: 1000 m

About

Objective

This interactive visualization is part of the Walking Safe capstone project of the discipline IA369 ministered at Unicamp. The task is a proof of concept of one of the modules that may be presented in the capstone.

Data

The original data was retrivied from the San Fransicso Police Department Incidents of 2016 in 05/08/2017. I want to give a special thanks to the DataSF website for providing not only this, but also a wide range of open datasets for the public.

The data is prepared in a Jupyter notebook using Python. All the data and source code is available at this temporary repository.
